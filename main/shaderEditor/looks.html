Looks Blocks

"set colour to [colour]"

Sets the colour of the current pixel, or vertex to the desired colour.

"colour"

Returns the current colour.

"set [attribute] to [value]" and "change [attribute] by [value]"

Sets or changes the desired attribute to or by the desired value.
Attributes are

  • hue (0-360)
  • saturation (0-100)
  • value (0-100)
  • red (0-255)
  • green (0-255)
  • blue (0-255)
  • alpha (0-255)

"apply multiplicative blending"

Makes transparency blend instead of brighten pixels behind it.

About UV coordinates

Before moving on it is important to understand how UV coordinates work.

UV coordinates range from 0-1 with U being X and V being Y.
Commonly (0,0) is the top-left and (1,1) is the bottom-right of the image, but it should be known that Scratch's costumes have (0,1) as the top-left corner and (1,0) is the bottom-right.

The UV blocks

These blocks allow you to modify, and get the UV coordinates of the pixel or vertex.
Most of the time you will probably be using "current uv".

color at [uv] of texture [texture]

Allows you to get the colour of a texture at a desired uv coordinate.

color at [direction] of cubemap [cubemap]

Allows you to get the colour of a direction in a cubemap.

color at [uvw] of 3D texture [texture]

Allows you to get the colour of a texture at a desired uv coordinate, but with a third dimension.
